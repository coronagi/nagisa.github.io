Semtech Coop — QA / Test Engineer (EM Series Modules)
Software test engineer validating cellular IoT modules across Windows and Linux environments.

Company & Product Context

Semtech provides wireless communication technologies and IoT products. During my co-op, I worked with the EM series cellular communication modules (including the compact, high-performance EM92 series), designed for high-speed data communication and multi-band support across regions and networks.

My Role

As a QA / Test Engineer, I tested EM9291 and EM9293 modules, focusing on host software (drivers and connectivity applications) and device firmware on both Windows and Linux. I executed release test cases, reported defects, and verified fixes through structured verification and validation workflows.

Goals

  • Build practical QA skills and become fluent in the end-to-end testing workflow
  • Use tools effectively: JIRA (bug tracking), qTest (test management), and automation frameworks
  • Improve testing efficiency and accuracy through automation and repeatable procedures

Test Process

The testing process begins with a test request from the test planner (including the JIRA request, deadline, and qTest information). Test cases are executed according to the steps documented in qTest, then marked as Passed, Blocked, Incomplete, or Failed. Based on results, a new build may be generated, and previously passed tests may be omitted in subsequent cycles unless specified.

Ticket verification is also tracked in JIRA. Both build (driver tests) and integrated (firmware tests) must be completed by the deadline, ensuring issues are reproducible and fixes are validated.

Tools & Responsibilities

JIRA (Bug Tracking)

  • Create and track tickets with clear reproduction steps and context
  • Attach logs/screenshots to support investigation and resolution
  • Communicate concisely in ticket discussions to align with developers and test planners

Reliability Stress Testing

  • Run long-duration, high-load tests to evaluate durability and reliability
  • Analyze failures using logs/screenshots to separate bugs vs. environment/setup issues
  • Reconfigure test environments and rerun tests when setup issues are identified

Challenges & How I Addressed Them

Challenge What made it hard How I handled it
Complex ticket verification Past issues and interrelated problems created large amounts of logs and context to review Reviewed explanations/comments carefully, asked experienced teammates for guidance, and clarified via Teams
Bug vs. setup/environment issues Device enumeration and PCIe-related issues could look like defects but were sometimes setup problems Started with logs, checked existing tickets, validated on different machines, and tightened reproduction conditions
Long-running stress tests Tests could run up to two weeks; failures were difficult to triage quickly Iterated systematically: isolate signals, rerun after environment reset, and document conditions in detail

Accomplishments

  • Became familiar with Semtech’s testing workflow and QA tooling (JIRA, qTest)
  • Gained hands-on experience with automation and reliability stress testing
  • Improved technical communication by writing clearer tickets and attaching actionable evidence (logs/screenshots)
  • Experienced a real Canadian work environment and strengthened professional collaboration habits

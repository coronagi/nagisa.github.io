Sado, and what it taught me

Sado (the Japanese tea ceremony) has quietly shaped how I approach awareness, mindfulness, and daily life. Through years of practice, I learned to value stillness, careful attention, and the subtle beauty found in the tea room— from seasonal decorations to the utensils chosen for each gathering.

The tea room is a place where time slows down. Each movement has meaning, and each detail is intentional. That atmosphere continues to influence how I live and how I reset my mind in a fast-paced world.

Wabi-Yoga

These experiences inspired what I call “Wabi-Yoga”: a personal practice of stepping away from digital distractions, calming the mind, and creating intentional time for reflection. It’s not about perfection—it's about returning to simplicity, presence, and quiet clarity.

How I practice

  • Reduce noise: silence, minimal space, no notifications
  • Slow down: breathe, soften the posture, settle the gaze
  • Notice details: light, sound, season, texture, temperature

What it supports

  • Grounding during busy work or study periods
  • A deeper sense of gratitude and attention
  • Respect for craft, tradition, and everyday beauty

About Sohen-ryu (宗偏流)

Sohen-ryu (宗偏流) is a school of Japanese tea ceremony with a history that traces back over centuries in Japan. Like other tea traditions, it carries a lineage of aesthetics, etiquette, and philosophy—teaching practitioners to cultivate sensitivity, humility, and care through the act of preparing and sharing tea.

Receiving my tea name: “Sosa (宗沙)”

Recently, I received the tea name “Sosa (宗沙)” from the master of the Sohen-ryu school.

In Sado, a tea name is given as part of the tradition and carries the spirit of the lineage. I feel deeply grateful for this moment, and for the opportunity to continue learning within this cultural practice.

Values I carry forward

The values of harmony, respect, purity, and tranquility found in tea continue to influence both my personal life and how I approach the world—quietly, consistently, and with care.
